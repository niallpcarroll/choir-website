Gregorian Chant

Here are some helpful resources for Gregorian chant:

  • Gregorian Chant Hymns

    Gregorian Chant Hymns has an extensive collection of sheet music and recordings of some of the most popular plainchant for the liturgy.

  • Reading & Singing Gregorian Chant

    Corpus Christi Watershed has an extensive page with eleven lessons for those wishing to learn how to read and sing Gregorian chant.

  • Jubilate Deo

    Pope Paul VI sent a copy of Jubilate Deo to every bishop in the world in April 1974. It contains what was intended to be the minimum plainchant repertoire that the faithful should be able to sing.

Church Documents on Sacred Music

Here are the most relevant Church documents dealing with the subject of sacred music:

  • Tra le Sollecitudini

    Issued by Pope St Pius X in 1903, Tra le Sollecitudini was a key document in the revival of Gregorian chant, and it contains an excellent explanation of what sacred music is.

  • Musicae Sacrae Disciplina

    The 1955 encyclical of Ven. Pope Pius XII, Musicae sacrae disciplina, reaffirms the duty of Church musicians to preserve Latin plainchant and to preserve the dignity of sacred music in general.

  • De Musica Sacra

    The Sacred Congregation of Rites issued De musica sacra in September 1958. Apart from reaffirming previous norms, it deals with topical issues such as sound amplification, the use of "automatic" instruments and pre-recorded music.

  • Sacrosanctum Concilium

    Sacrosanctum concilium was the first document issued by the Second Vatican Council. Although it deals with the sacred liturgy in general, Chapter VI deals specifically with sacred music. Although sometimes cited as a revolutionary document, it largely reaffirms the teachings of previous pontiffs.

  • Musicam Sacram

    Issued by the Sacred Congregation of Rites in 1967, Musicam sacram is the last major document issued by the Church on the subject of sacred music. It looks at questions such as the place of the vernacular in sacred music, the use of Gregorian chant, musical instruments, and the "hierarchy" of sung texts in the liturgy.
